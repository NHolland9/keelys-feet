Foot Health Services

Below is a list of foot health issues I regularly treat. Click on the tabs below for more information about each one.

Toenail Trimming

Trimming your toenails may seem simple enough, but it isn't something to be done carelessly. While most people don't need to visit a professional to take care of their toes some may need to be sure they're clipping their nails properly to reduce the risk of future foot problems. You depend on your feet, so make sure you give them the care they deserve.

Diabetic Foot Care

It's especially important to look after your feet if you have diabetes. Diabetes can reduce the blood supply to your feet and cause a loss of feeling known as peripheral neuropathy. This can mean foot injuries don't heal well, and you may not notice if your foot is sore or injured.

Corns

Corns are areas of hard, thickened skin that develop when the skin is exposed to excessive pressure or friction. They commonly occur on the feet and can cause pain and discomfort when you walk. They are more common among people who wear ill-fitting shoes, have sweaty feet, and those who stand for long periods each day

Cracked Heels

Cracked skin results from tiny splits that occur in dry, damaged skin. If left untreated, these tears can grow into deeper cracks; also known as fissures. A common area for cracks to appear is the heel as it carries the weight of the body whenever you stand. Without regular removal of dry skin and a lack of moisture, cracked heels could appear more often.

Hard Skin Removal

Hard skin forms as yellowish rough patches that often occur around the heel or on the ball of the foot. It is the body’s protection mechanism to stop the foot developing a sore or wound. The body continues to produce hard skin when you don’t need it and this can become painful. If left untreated the skin can become dry, swollen or even crack.

Verrucas

A verruca is a viral infection of the skin that is often picked up through direct contact with moist surfaces containing the virus such as swimming pools and changing rooms. Once the verruca is embedded in the skin, it will often accelerate the turnover of skin which can cause discomfort. In some cases, a verruca can become painful, and interfere with your daily activities.
